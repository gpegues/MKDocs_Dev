# Calendar Options in Microsoft 365: Pros and Cons

## Outlook Calendar
### Pros
- **Familiarity:** Widely used and familiar to most users.
- **Integration with Email:** Direct link with email for invites and updates.
- **Permission Control:** Ability to set different permission levels.

### Cons
- **Performance Issues:** [Performance can degrade](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/answers/questions/443418/microsoft-outlook-is-there-a-limit-to-the-number-o.html) with too many users (more than 25 not recommended for a shared mailbox).
- **Manageability:** More challenging to manage with a large number of users.

## Microsoft Teams
### Pros
- **Large Team Support:** Can support [up to 10,000 members](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-teams/group-chat-software) in a single team.
- **Collaboration Features:** Built for collaboration and communication within teams.
- **Integration with M365 Tools:** Seamless integration with other Microsoft 365 tools.

### Cons
- **Dependent on Team Limits:** Limited by the team and channel capacities in Teams.
- **Potentially Complex for Simple Calendar Needs:** Might be more than needed for just calendar sharing.

## SharePoint Calendar
### Pros
- **High Scalability:** Can support [up to 2 million users](https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/sharepoint/online-service-description/sharepoint-online-limits) in a site collection.
- **Customization and Flexibility:** Highly customizable for specific needs.
- **Integration with SharePoint Features:** Can be integrated with other SharePoint functionalities.

### Cons
- **Learning Curve:** Might have a steeper learning curve for users not familiar with SharePoint.
- **Performance Dependent on Usage:** Performance varies based on usage and site data.
